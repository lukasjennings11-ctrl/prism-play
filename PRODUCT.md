# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Casual web gamers — people with a spare five minutes, on any device, who want to play something immediately without an install, account, or tutorial wall. Skew mobile. They arrive via link-share, portal discovery, or bookmark. Context: bored, transitional moments (commute, lunch, waiting). Not hardcore gamers; not children specifically. Anyone who recognizes the genre title and knows what they're in for.

## Product Purpose

Prism Play is a browser-native arcade that ships original casual games at high volume with zero friction. No installs. No accounts. No paywalls. Revenue comes from portal licensing (CrazyGames, Poki, GameDistribution). Success means: players click once and are in a game within seconds; they come back for the daily game (Equate); they share scores. Ten games in the initial lineup.

## Brand Personality

Sharp, immediate, unpretentious. Three words: focused · electric · effortless.

Voice: confident without being loud. Copy says exactly what the game does — no hype, no clichés, no "addictive" or "epic." The games are good; the copy doesn't need to oversell them.

## Anti-references

- App store aesthetics: fake star ratings, "⚡POPULAR" badges, blinking CTAs
- Generic mobile game portals: neon-on-black gradients, aggressive monetization language, every font weight at max
- "Cozy indie" aesthetic: muted pastels, hand-drawn serif, soft corners everywhere
- SaaS landing page: feature bullets, hero metrics, quote testimonials

## Design Principles

1. **Zero to game in one tap.** Every design choice that adds friction is wrong. Cards should feel like buttons, not previews.
2. **Each game earns its own color.** The portal is the host; each game has a distinct personality. Don't flatten them into one palette.
3. **Copy as design.** Short, factual, punchy. Taglines describe what you DO, not how you'll FEEL.
4. **Mobile as the true canvas.** Responsive is not "also works on mobile." Mobile is first; desktop is a bonus.
5. **Motion signals state, not style.** Stagger the cards once on load (orienting the user), then stop. Game feel lives inside the games.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Canvas games are inherently limited in a11y; compensate at the portal layer: keyboard-navigable cards, sufficient contrast on all text, focus-visible states, `prefers-reduced-motion` respected. Genre filter uses role="radiogroup" semantics.
